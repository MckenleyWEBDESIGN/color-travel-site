Getting Around

  1. The Tube (London Underground): The fastest way to travel
  2. Buses: A great way to see the city above ground.
  3. Black Cabs and Ride-Sharing: Readily available but can be expensive
  4. Bikes and Walking: Many areas are pedestrian-friendly.

London is packed with fun things to do, no matter what you're into. You can start your day by taking in the stunning city views from the top of the London Eye or, if you prefer something more historic, explore the Tower of London and get up close to the Crown Jewels. For a more relaxed vibe, wander through the colorful streets of Notting Hill or grab some street food at Borough Market. If you're a fan of the arts, catch a world-class show in the West End or check out the latest exhibitions at the Tate Modern. And of course, no trip to London is complete without a classic pub visit—find a cozy spot, grab a pint, and soak in the city's charm.

Food lovers can enjoy traditional British dishes like fish and chips, full English breakfast, and Sunday roast. Experience afternoon tea at The Ritz or Sketch, savor street food at Borough Market or Brick Lane, and don’t forget to visit a classic British pub, such as Ye Olde Cheshire Cheese.
